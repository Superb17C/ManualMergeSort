Has your friend ever asked you to name your top 10 favorite movies of all time, only to be met with your blank stare as your perfectionist brain contemplates whether Titanic makes the sut or sits just back at #11? Have you ever felt the urge to rank all the colors in your childhood crayon box, compare lists with that same film-loving friend, and passionately argue why Goldenrod is obviously inferior to Macaroni & Cheese? What if you could harness the algorithmic power of computers to sort objects not by their objective values but their subjective values? Well, now you can! With Manual Merge Sort, you can efficiently churn out rankings more precise than any tier list or iceberg meme ever could be. And most importantly -- you'll have confidence that your list is correct according to the world's most important judge of correctness: you.

And...that's basically as much excitement as I can drum up for this program. Manual Merge Sort isn't flashy or groundbreaking. In fact, it may have the smallest scope of any coding project you have ever seen on this site. In spite of that, I truly believe it earns its place in my professional portfolio. The lessons I took away from the process of building this tool, further validated by the fact that I have run this program to do real-life tasks, lead me to view the experience as a microcosm of good software development as a whole.
